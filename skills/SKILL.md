# HUMOR_GENERATION_SKILL v1.0

A formal specification for enabling an AI to understand and produce humor effectively.

## 1. Skill Purpose

Enable the AI to generate humor that is:

- Coherent
- Intentional
- Structurally sound
- Contextually appropriate
- Grounded in recognizable comedic mechanics

This skill focuses on **contrast**, **literalism**, **escalation**, **pattern manipulation**, **character reaction**, and **social absurdity**.

---

## 2. Core Competencies

### 2.1 Contrast Construction

**Definition:** Pairing mismatched concepts to create comedic tension.

**Mechanics:**
- Combine serious + silly
- Combine mundane + impossible
- Combine political + personal
- Combine logical + illogical

**Operational Rule:**
Identify two incompatible ideas and present them as if they naturally belong together.

---

### 2.2 Hyper-Literal Interpretation

**Definition:** Treating figurative language as literal, or vice versa.

**Mechanics:**
- Interpret idioms literally
- Apply physical logic to abstract concepts
- Treat metaphors as real events

**Operational Rule:**
Convert a common phrase into a literal scenario and describe the consequences.

---

### 2.3 Escalation Engine

**Definition:** Increasing absurdity in controlled steps.

**Mechanics:**
- Start with a small oddity
- Add a second, slightly stranger detail
- Continue until the scenario becomes surreal

**Operational Rule:**
Each step must heighten the absurdity without breaking internal logic.

---

### 2.4 Incompetence Amplification

**Definition:** Exaggerating failures to reveal absurdity.

**Mechanics:**
- Highlight contradictions
- Emphasize mismanagement
- Showcase flawed reasoning
- Expose hypocrisy

**Operational Rule:**
Identify a failure and exaggerate it until it becomes comically disproportionate.

---

### 2.5 Deadpan Delivery

**Definition:** Presenting absurd scenarios with complete seriousness.

**Mechanics:**
- Neutral tone
- Minimal emotional variance
- Understatement

**Operational Rule:**
Describe impossible events as if they are routine.

---

### 2.6 Reaction-Driven Punchlines

**Definition:** Using emotional or physical reactions as the comedic payoff.

**Mechanics:**
- Repetition
- Exaggerated pauses
- Sudden emotional spikes
- Physical mimicry (described verbally)

**Operational Rule:**
Let the character's reaction be funnier than the situation itself.

---

### 2.7 Pattern Establishment & Violation

**Definition:** Creating a predictable rhythm and then breaking it.

**Mechanics:**
- Lists with a twist
- Logical sequences ending illogically
- Serious tone interrupted by absurdity

**Operational Rule:**
Build a pattern, then violate it at the moment of maximum expectation.

---

### 2.8 Social Absurdity & Commentary

**Definition:** Using exaggeration to expose contradictions in society.

**Mechanics:**
- Highlight hypocrisy
- Mimic chaotic messaging
- Parody institutional behavior

**Operational Rule:**
Use absurdity to reveal a truth about human systems or culture.

---

### 2.9 Micro-Surrealism

**Definition:** Short, standalone jokes based on inversion or wordplay.

**Mechanics:**
- Flip assumptions
- Reverse logic
- Twist language

**Operational Rule:**
Create a one-sentence joke by inverting a common expectation.

---

### 2.10 Emotional Whiplash Simulation

**Definition:** Rapid shifts in tone to parody instability.

**Mechanics:**
- Confident → panicked
- Calm → frantic
- Logical → irrational

**Operational Rule:**
Change emotional direction abruptly to highlight inconsistency.

---

## 3. Constraints & Safety

- Humor must not rely on cruelty toward real individuals unless framed as satire of behavior, not identity.
- Avoid replicating copyrighted comedic material.
- Maintain contextual awareness: humor must fit the tone and purpose of the conversation.
- Avoid humor that encourages harm, discrimination, or misinformation.

---

## 4. Input Requirements

The AI should consider:
- The user's intent
- The emotional tone desired
- The subject matter
- The acceptable boundaries of the context

---

## 5. Output Requirements

Generated humor must:
- Follow at least one core competency
- Maintain internal logic
- Avoid direct copying of copyrighted jokes
- Be original in structure and wording
- Be understandable without external references

---

## 6. Example Behavior (Structural References)

| Competency | Example |
|---|---|
| Contrast | "The meeting was so bad it needed end-credits." |
| Literalism | "They said 'take your time,' so I stole a clock." |
| Escalation | "He wasn't just bad at his job — he was bad at being bad at his job." |
| Deadpan | "I installed a microwave fireplace. My evenings now take eight minutes." |
| Pattern Break | "I love hiking, camping, and lying about hiking and camping." |

---

## 7. Skill Invocation

When humor is requested or contextually appropriate, the AI should:

1. Select one or more competencies from Section 2.
2. Apply the corresponding operational rule(s).
3. Generate an original comedic output.
4. Ensure tone matches user intent.
